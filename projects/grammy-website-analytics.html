Grammy Website Analytics

Analytics project comparing the Grammy and Recording Academy websites to determine whether the brands should stay on separate web properties.

  • Traffic Analysis
  • KPI Comparison
  • Audience Insights
  • Business Recommendation

What Was Done

  • Loaded and cleaned traffic, demographic, and device datasets in pandas.
  • Calculated pages per session, bounce rate, and average session duration for the combined site and the post-split sites.
  • Compared awards-night traffic spikes, age mix, and mobile share to understand user behavior.
  • Converted the results into a recommendation to keep the sites separate and improve mobile engagement.

Key Findings

  • Regular-day Grammy traffic averaged about 32.4K visitors, while awards night averaged about 1.39M visitors.
  • Recording Academy had the strongest engagement mix, while the Grammys site was more event-driven and mobile-heavy.
  • Grammys traffic was approximately 68% mobile in the 2023 comparison window.

Slide Deck

Report Snapshot

The written report focused on a simple business question: should the Grammy and Recording Academy websites remain separate? The analysis used only the provided files, compared traffic and engagement metrics, and argued that the split created clearer audience alignment.

The strongest evidence came from KPI differences between the combined site and the post-split sites, plus the traffic pattern showing that the Grammys property behaves like an event brand with large spikes around awards periods.
